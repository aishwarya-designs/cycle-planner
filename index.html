Why each hormone matters

Estrogen
Drives energy, mood, and libido. Peaks before ovulation. Its crash in the late luteal phase is the primary driver of mood dips and the feeling that everything is harder than it should be.
Progesterone
Rises after ovulation — mildly sedating, raises body temperature, increases appetite by 100–300 kcal/day. Makes the gym feel impossible. But also quiets mental noise — deep focus improves.
LH (Luteinising Hormone)
Surges sharply at ovulation to trigger egg release. Associated with the confidence, verbal fluency, and social magnetism many women feel around day 14.
FSH (Follicle Stimulating)
Rises at the start of the cycle to stimulate follicle growth — this drives the energy recovery after your period. A second FSH rise at ovulation works alongside the LH surge.
