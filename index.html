How you might feel

🌊
Menstrual · Days 1–5
Inward & tender
Bleeding begins. Energy is low. You may feel tired, crampy, and emotionally sensitive. The urge to slow down and stay home is physiologically driven — not laziness. Rest is productive here.
🌱
Follicular · Days 6–13
Energised & optimistic
Estrogen is climbing. Mood lifts naturally. Motivation, creativity, and physical capacity all improve week by week. You feel like yourself again — curious, social, and ready to take things on.
✨
Ovulatory · Days 14–16
Confident & magnetic
LH surges. Estrogen peaks. Testosterone briefly rises. You feel your most articulate, confident, and sociable. Verbal fluency and charisma are measurably higher. This is your biological performance peak.
🌙
Luteal · Days 17–28
Deep & depleted
Progesterone rises then crashes. Serotonin falls. Physical energy drops, hunger increases by 100–300 kcal. The gym feels impossible. But mental focus and depth improve — your best solo deep work happens here.

Why each hormone matters

Estrogen
Drives energy, mood, and libido. Peaks before ovulation — when you feel most yourself. Its crash in the late luteal phase is the primary driver of mood dips, low motivation, and the familiar feeling that everything is harder than it should be.
Progesterone
Rises after ovulation — mildly sedating, raises body temperature, increases appetite by 100–300 kcal/day. Its sedating effect is why the gym feels impossible in the late luteal phase. But it also quiets mental noise — deep focus actually improves.
LH (Luteinising Hormone)
Surges sharply at ovulation to trigger egg release. Brief but powerful. The LH peak is associated with the confidence, verbal fluency, and social magnetism many women notice around day 14. It's your biological spotlight moment.
FSH (Follicle Stimulating)
Rises at the start of the cycle to stimulate follicle growth — this is what drives the energy recovery after your period. A secondary FSH rise at ovulation works alongside the LH surge to complete the process.
